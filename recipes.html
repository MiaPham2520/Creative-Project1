As an international student, I really miss my home country food when I study abroad. Consequently, I spend a lot of my time finding receipes of the food I like and try cooking them. Below are some of my favorite receipes that I often cook for myself and my friends.

Bun Moc

Photo retrieved from CookBeo

What is Bun Moc?

Bun moc (vermicelli with meatballs) is a easy dish to cook. What take the most time is simmering the pork bones to get the sweet broth tase. If you don't have time to simmer the bones, pre-made chicken stock is a good alternation. When winter comes, this dish become extremely popular because people are seeking something warm to fill their tummy.

Ingredients

The ingredients I included below are what I often use to make bun moc. If you look at other receipes, there may be slight differences.

  • Pork bones
  • Minced pork
  • Wood ear mushroom
  • Shitake mushroom
  • Taro petiole (optional)
  • Vermicelli

You can follow this receipe to make your own bun moc. Don't forget to adjust the receipe and ingredients to fit your taste!

Nem (Fried Spring Rolls)

Photo retrieved from Delightful Plate

What is Fried Spring Roll?

Spring rolls can be a bit time consuming to make since you have to prep all the ingredients and wrap them yourself. You can choose to buy pre-wrapped ones at the market, but Vietnamese usually make their own since each family will slighty alter the ingredients. This is a dish where you will mostly see at big holidays or festivals like Lunar New Year.

Ingredients

These are ingredients that I use when making their spring rolls. There may be other variations depending on which region of Vietnam you are from.

  • Glass noodles
  • Carrot
  • Yellow onion
  • Wood ear mushroom
  • Shitake mushroom
  • Minced pork
  • Egg
